The agriculture sector contributes 51 percent of Kenyas GDP and accounts for 60 percent of employment and 65 percent of exports. The sector is dominated by smallholder production on farms of between 0.2 and 3 hectares, which account for 78 percent of total agricultural production and 70 percent of commercial production. Between 2009 and 2014, the percentage of landholdings held by urban households increased from 22 percent to 32.1 percent. The proportion of Kenyan farmers who receive extension advice is low. Ethiopia has one extension agent for every 72 farmers.
