90% of sunflowers grown in Canada are grown in Manitoba, but a growing share is finding its way into southeastern Saskatchewan, southern Alberta and southern Ontario. 65% of all Canadian sunflower plants are confection types marketed as roasted snack food in the shell or as dehulled seeds for the baking industry. Oilseed sunflower are used in both the birdfood and the crushing industry for sunflower oil producing one of the highest quality vegetable oils.
