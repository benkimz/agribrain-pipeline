According to Vsquez-Alvarado et al. (2008), at the highland area of Zacatecas-San Lus Potos, Mexico, the main cultivated nopal species are: O. leucotricha (duraznillo nopal), O. streptacantha (card nopal) and O. robusta (tapn nopal). In the case of prickly pear fruits (tunas), the state of San Luis Potos has 416 ha of cultivated nopales. The state ranks sixth at the Mexican level, with a production extension of 388 ha of vegetable nopal (medium growth cladodes).
